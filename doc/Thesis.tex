\abstract{
\addtocontents{toc}{\vspace{1em}}  % Add a gap in the Contents, for aesthetics

This research lies in the domain of Outdoor Mixed Reality. It aims to visualize the viewing fields of outdoor surveillance cameras on the screen of mobile devices. In an outdoor environment where the camera on the mobile devices can be used, it is required that the users can easily understands the visualized viewing fields of the surrounding surveillance cameras. Moreover, this visualization system must provide realtime video with no human-sensible delay.

In this research, we study five visualization methods and propose a prototype implementation based on server-side PTAM. The prototype provides realtime video and requires no GPS and gyrocompass devices.

The structure of this thesis:

\begin{itemize}
	\item Chapter \ref{Chapter1} introduces the need of visualizing viewing fields of surveillance cameras.
	\item Chapter \ref{Chapter2} puts this research in a background of related researches.
	\item Chapter \ref{Chapter3} poses basic requirements for visualization methods, then studies five methods and algorithms to implement them.
	\item Chapter \ref{Chapter4} propose a prototype based on server-side PTAM which implements and integrates the five methods in chapter \ref{Chapter3}.
	\item Chapter \ref{Chapter5} uses the prototype to conduct an experiment to evaluate the five methods in the context of the prototype, then discusses the result.
	\item Chapter \ref{Chapter6} summarizes and proposes future directions for this research.
\end{itemize}
